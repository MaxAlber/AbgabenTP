Author

Anthony Burgess (John Anthony Burgess Wilson) was an English writer and composer. Burgess grew up in a catholic family in Manchester and became one of the best known English literary figures of the twentieth century. Burgess was a comic writer but his dystopian satire A Clockwork Orange was his greatest hit and remains his best known novel.

Burgess also composed over 250 musical works. He saw himself as much as an musician as an author, although he had more success in writing.

Characters

Alex:

Alex is the Main-character in our story. He is portrayed as a sociopath who robs, rapes, and assaults innocent people for his own amusement. He also knows that his behaviour is morally wrong.

Dim and Pete:

Dim and Pete are two friends of Alex that betray him and because of them Alex has to go to the jail. Later in the book they become Police Officers and meet Alex again at the End of the book

Prison Chaplain:

The resident priest at Alex’s prison. He preaches to the prisoners about morals. He gives the most clear expression of the importance of free will.

Dr.Brodsky:

The State-employed behavioral scientist in charge of administering Ludovico’s Technique to Alex.

Story

Protagonist Alex DeLarge is an "ultraviolent" youth in futuristic Britain. As with all luck, his eventually runs out and he's arrested and convicted of murder and rape. While in prison, Alex learns of an experimental program in which convicts are programmed to detest violence. If he goes through the program, his sentence will be reduced and he will be back on the streets sooner than expected.

But Alex's ordeals are far from over once he hits the mean streets of Britain that he had a hand in creating.

Place and Time

A Clockwork Orange takes place in futuristic Britain. The specific year is not mentioned, but it is assumed that the story takes place in the not-so-distant future. The government is run by a totalitarian super-state. Individual rights are not protected. The youth of society have formed a culture of violence.

Writing Style

A very important factor is the clever and unique style of Burgess in using Nadsat, a very special slang. the very second sentence of Chapter 1 shows just how clever the text as a whole can be:

There was me, that is Alex, and my three droogs, that is Pete, Georgie, and Dim, Dim being really dim, and we sat in the Korova Milkbar making up our rassoodocks what to do with the evening, a flip dark chill winter bastard though dry. (1.1.2).

Critic on the Society

I think the critical view on society, or modern society, is the most important part of the book. Anthony Burgess wants us to think about how modern society is supressing our free will. It also critizises the authorities of our society, and the fact that we are accepting the suppression of our free will. In the definition of the book, the rules of our society are making us to blind slaves, who are no longer able to act free.

It wants to tell us, that every human being has a bad side, but its suppressed by our conditioning. Now we have to decide for ourselves if its good that we lost our ability to decide by free will.

Review

I really much enjoyed reading the book, most because of its critical view on our world and society. Although I liked to read it, its a bit hard to understand because of the slang thats used in the book. Its called "Nadsat" and its completely made up by the author of the book, Anthony Burgess. Its a mixture of anglicized Russian words and Cockney rhyming slang.

In case you want to read the book, I suggest you to take an easier version of the book and not the original one from Anthony Burgess. Or you could also watch the movie (one of the greatest movies of all time in my eyes).
